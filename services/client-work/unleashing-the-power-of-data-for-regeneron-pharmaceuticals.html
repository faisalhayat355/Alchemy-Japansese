Unleashing the Power of Data for Regeneron Pharmaceuticals


How EPAM Continuum Partnered with Regeneron to Design a First-of-Its-Kind Data Literacy Program

SHARE
  • link copied

AT A GLANCE

CLIENT

Regeneron Pharmaceuticals

SERVICES

Data & Analytics

Strategy

Talent Enablement

INDUSTRY

Life Sciences & Healthcare

For 35 years, the mission of the biotechnology company Regeneron has been to use the power of science to bring new medicines to patients with serious diseases. Given how data and analytics can accelerate the speed of delivery — from early discovery to manufacturing and commercialization — Regeneron knew that the stronger its data literacy, the faster it could bring medicines to patients. Regeneron partnered with EPAM Continuum to design an innovative data literacy program to power more data-driven insights.

DEFINING OBJECTIVES

Embrace and Cultivate a Data-Literate Workforce

As a leading research-based pharmaceutical company, Regeneron produces and analyzes vast amounts of data every day — data that is growing exponentially in volume, velocity and variety. Regeneron leadership recognized that a data-literate workforce — one that uses data as a company asset in decision-making, evaluates and questions data, knows how to find data, and confidently interacts with data to derive insights and tell a story about it — is critical to business success.

To grow data maturity in the company, Regeneron defined three key data literacy objectives:

Enable employees to drive business growth and operational efficiencies by leveraging data as an enterprise asset
Develop the skills needed to understand, work with, collaborate and communicate using data
Drive awareness and increase adoption and innovative uses of data platforms

A THREE-PRONGED STRATEGY

Developing the First-of-Its-Kind Data Literacy Program

To achieve its goal, Regeneron partnered with EPAM Continuum to create a three-pronged strategy that leverages shared frameworks and methodologies from The Data Lodge®:

Design a data literacy program that drives awareness and engagement, raises data literacy levels across the enterprise and supports employees in embracing data literacy in their daily jobs
Pilot and launch data literacy programs aimed at small groups at Regeneron
Continuously expand and evolve data literacy efforts while supporting a growing data literacy alumni community

The resulting program was one of the first of its kind, with Regeneron on the leading edge of a global trend.

Program Highlights

Data Maturity Assessment


The first step on Regeneron’s journey was to examine its own data literacy maturity. While Regeneron already used data well, an assessment unveiled key improvement opportunities. EPAM leveraged these findings to structure the data literacy program, including building a common language around data and cultivating a data-informed culture where people understand how to access data and connect it to value.

Role-Based Personalization


As part of the assessment, we discovered that Regeneron employees had varying degrees of data literacy. Additionally, each role at Regeneron required different levels of data literacy and had nuances specific to that role. To ensure the program was successful, we provided targeted, relevant content that was aligned with each participant’s unique role and knowledge level.

Learning Content


Leveraging our own learning platforms and content, EPAM Continuum designed a 16-week program that starts with a kickoff week and then the remaining time is divided into five modules:​ 1) introduction to data literacy, 2) data policies and governance​, 3) data tools and data sources, 4) fundamentals of data analytics and 5) communicating with data.​ Each module consists of two weeks of individual self-paced e-learning and a third week of virtual webinars. The content combines curated learning videos with relevant in-house expertise through user-generated content at Regeneron. The program is presented through a variety of online modalities in a responsive format including interactions (flip cards, tabs, accordions, carousels, etc.), knowledge checks, application activities, data projects, assessments and facilitated virtual sessions.

Learning Amplification


To make the data literacy program truly transformative, we created a data literacy platform that serves as a hub for participants to gain up-to-date information on the program, latest data literacy news, frequently asked questions and other resources. Additionally, the team built a competency matrix, which aligns desired data literacy knowledge with roles throughout the company to ensure employees have the expertise required to help Regeneron reach its data literacy objectives.

Data-Driven Problem Solving


As one of the interactive elements of the program, we developed a workbook that allows participants to reflect on challenges or opportunities in their personal or professional lives and how they can leverage data to solve them effectively. Participants describe the challenge in terms of what they hoped to gain or resolve by applying data-informed decision-making. Sample scenarios are provided for users to reference as they work through each step of their chosen challenge.

Community & Recognition


Each module includes the opportunity to continue the conversation with peers on Microsoft Teams. This helps build a data culture and community by empowering participants to put their newfound skills into practice and share their insights so others could learn. ​And the connections don’t stop there — we developed a data literacy alumni program to enable alums to actively contribute and gain recognition for using their data literacy skills to understand, work with and communicate with data, treat data as an asset and identify business use cases for getting value out of the data.

POSITIVE FEEDBACK FROM PARTICIPANTS

“This first training module is very impressive — maybe the best training module I've seen in my 10 years at Regeneron. Content is clear, concise and has been scoped appropriately based on existing personal knowledge reported by each trainee. You can find an estimated completion time for each section, a breadcrumb trail showing progress, videos with adjustable playback speed and an editable workbook for ongoing consistent learning and engagement. I had no technical issues, I was able to leave my training and return back to it, and I wasn’t overwhelmed by knowledge checks.”

A NEW WAY OF WORKING

Embracing a Data-Literate Workforce

The program’s success is shown in the way Regeneron colleagues approach their projects, collaborate with data and speak about data in their daily work lives today. Here are some examples:

  • One participant immediately began applying their knowledge in meetings​.
  • The Senior Director of Portfolio Management, whose team participated in the program, has been able to make better data-informed decisions by analyzing archived data and integrating it with new data, scrubbing the information and learning about project staffing, resourcing and other factors that impact project delivery. 
  • A Lead Business Analyst reported that participating in the program made her think more about the data within her projects and recognize what adjustments should be made to align data to be more consistent for a true comparison so she could leverage data as an enterprise asset and ensure regulatory compliance.

Moreover, the impact of the program can be seen in the momentum it has gained across the organization and its organic growth to other groups from research and pre-clinical development, regulatory affairs and medical affairs to global safety.
